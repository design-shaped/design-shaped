# Role Family — UX Researcher

## Who this preset is for

This is the role-family preset for designers whose resumes need to read as **UX researcher** — dedicated research roles, mixed research-design roles where research is the headline, research-ops roles, and senior IC researchers targeting principal or staff research. In v1 scope, "UX Researcher" covers the full IC track at companies that hire research as a discipline (not folded into product or design generalist roles). For research-leaning generalists whose target role is product designer with research, route to the Product Designer preset and surface research methods inside bullet How clauses; this preset is for resumes whose top-of-page positioning is *researcher* and whose bullet evidence has to back that. The bar for the preset: research drove a decision, prevented a mistake, or shifted what the team thought was true — and the bullet says so.

---

## Section 1 — Verb set

UXR-target action verbs (FEATURES §2.1 UXR row + corpus extension): *synthesized, recruited, instrumented, triangulated, moderated, conducted, validated, identified, surfaced, influenced.* Each anchors a research-grade action — *synthesized* names the cross-study analytic move, *recruited* names the sample-side work, *instrumented* names quantitative-research scaffolding, *triangulated* names mixed-methods convergence, *moderated* names live-session craft, *validated* and *invalidated* both name the decision-impact outcome (FEATURES §2.1: *"Research drove a decision or prevented a mistake"*).

**Anti-set — banned AI-default verbs (voice.md §4 / §11.1, D-17-RR).** Never emit on the rewrite side: *spearheaded, leveraged, synergized, catalyzed, orchestrated, championed, drove* (without object), *amplified* (without metric). On a UXR resume the inflation tell is especially loud — *"spearheaded research initiatives"* signals LLM polish and abstracts away the methods, sample, and decision that earned the bullet its weight. The abstraction-regression guard from voice.md §4 applies: a UXR rewrite that drops the method name, the n=, or the decision-impact in service of a punchier verb has lost.

---

## Section 2 — Scope vocabulary

UXR scope language is method-and-decision-shaped, not product-surface-shaped (which is the PD preset's vocabulary). What scope looks like on a UXR bullet:

- **Study type:** generative interviews, evaluative usability tests, diary studies, longitudinal field studies, concept tests, card sorts, tree tests, RITE iterations, unmoderated remote tests, contextual inquiry, ethnographic observation.
- **Sample (n=):** "n=18 moderated interviews," "n=240 survey respondents," "n=12 over 4-week diary study," "8 enterprise admin interviews + 24 end-user observations." The n= is load-bearing — a UXR bullet without it is missing scope evidence the way a PD bullet without surface size is.
- **Methodology rigor signals:** "screened to [criteria]," "stratified across [segments]," "triangulated against [analytics / support tickets / sales calls]," "pre-registered analysis plan," "double-coded qualitative themes."
- **Stakeholder reach:** "shared findings with PM, eng lead, and 2 designers," "presented to product leadership," "embedded findings in 3 product squads' planning," "informed Q3 OKRs across 4 teams."
- **Decision impact:** "team killed the [feature]," "scope cut from 9 stories to 4," "roadmap reshuffled to surface [problem] first," "shipped vs. didn't ship," "go/no-go on the [bet]." This is the senior-grade scope claim — see voice.md §11.3 *what changes because of you?* and Section 3 below.

## Section 3 — Common over-claims (UXR-specific patterns)

Three over-claim shapes recur on UXR resumes. Each is a positioning miss before it's a maturity miss; voice.md Pair 7 framing applies (calibration, not accusation).

**Over-claim 1 — Sole influence on a decision research informed.** Bullet reads "Drove the decision to kill the personalization feature based on research findings." UXR rarely *drives* a decision; UXR surfaces the evidence that the PM or product lead then weighs. The honest verb is *informed, surfaced, influenced* — and the bullet earns weight by naming the decision-maker who acted: "Surfaced 4 sessions of consistent confusion in the personalization onboarding; PM cut the feature from Q3 scope." Cites: **R12** (claiming team win solo — UXR variant), **R10** (seniority mismatch — over-claim direction). STREAM_MINING_RESUME.md §B1 verbatim Nate (4tg1bPbOxus L762–765) anchors the discipline: *"You're not gonna claim sole ownership of [a project that wasn't yours alone]."*

**Over-claim 2 — Research as the cause of a downstream metric.** Bullet attaches a clean conversion or revenue percentage to the research itself: "Research increased conversion by 22%." The research surfaced findings; the design and engineering team shipped a fix; the metric moved post-launch. Triangulating UXR's contribution to a revenue metric is genuinely hard, and clean attribution claims read as fabricated to recruiters who've worked with research teams. Honest framing: "Research findings informed the [redesign]; post-launch conversion improved 22% (Mixpanel, Q4 2024) — directional, not isolated to research contribution." Cites: **R02** (metric fabrication risk — attribution variant). voice.md §9.1 / §11.2 (No-fabricated-metrics rule) extends to fabricated *attribution*, not just fabricated numbers.

**Over-claim 3 — Methodology inflation.** Bullet describes a 6-participant unmoderated test as "longitudinal mixed-methods study with triangulated quantitative validation." The methodology vocabulary is real; the application isn't. Senior UXRs reading the bullet pattern-match the inflation instantly — the participant count and timeframe don't support the methodology label. Honest framing names the actual study shape: "6-participant unmoderated usability test on the [feature] prototype; findings triangulated against support-ticket sample (n=80, prior quarter)." Cites: **R10** (seniority mismatch — methodology over-claim variant), **R11** (process over-narration — when method-listing replaces honest scope).

**Over-claim 4 — Solo ownership of multi-researcher work.** Bullet reads "Led research strategy across 6 product surfaces" when the actual contribution was running 2 of 8 studies on a research-team-of-4. Same shape as **R12** but in the research-team variant: scope inflation that flattens the research team's structure. Fix: name the actual contribution scope ("contributed 2 of 8 studies on the v3 redesign research program; led the [Auth flow] usability arc within it"). STREAM_MINING_RESUME.md §B1 anchors.

## Section 4 — Common under-claims (UXR-specific patterns)

Senior UXR resumes under-claim more often than they over-claim — same root failure as PITFALLS §1.6 senior under-claiming, but with UXR-specific shapes.

**Under-claim 1 — Burying methodological rigor.** Bullet says "Conducted user interviews to understand checkout abandonment." Reads as method roster (R11 territory). What the bullet didn't surface: the screening criteria, the n=, the recruitment shape, the analysis approach. A UXR reviewer reading the bullet can't tell whether this was 4 hallway tests or 18 stratified moderated interviews. Fix: surface the rigor explicitly — "Conducted 18 moderated interviews stratified across new vs. returning users (screened on past-30-day cart abandonment); double-coded thematic analysis surfaced 3 dominant friction patterns." Cites: **R11** (process over-narration — under-claim direction, where methods are *too thin* not too verbose), **R10** (seniority mismatch — under-claim variant).

**Under-claim 2 — Omitting study scope and sample size.** Bullet reads "Validated the new onboarding flow through user testing." No n=, no method type, no segment. The bullet evidences research happened; it doesn't evidence the *scope* of research that happened. UXR resumes live or die on the n= — voice.md §5 (quote the user's actual work) extends here: rewrites surface the missing n= as an explicit probe, never invent it. Fix template: "[study type] with n=[count] [participants/segment]; findings [decision impact]." Cites: **R01** (activity bullet, no outcome — under-claim shape), **R02** (probe for the n=, never fabricate).

**Under-claim 3 — Hiding decision-impact outcomes.** Bullet stops at the research deliverable: "Delivered a research report on enterprise admin pain points." What's missing: what the team *did* with the report. UXR's senior-grade signal is decision-impact (FEATURES §2.1 UXR row: *"research drove a decision or prevented a mistake"*) — without it, the bullet reads as a methods-and-deliverables list, positioning the candidate at mid regardless of years. Apply voice.md §11.3 *what changes because of you?* — surface what the team did, what shipped, what didn't ship, or what the team learned to stop assuming. Fix: "Delivered enterprise admin pain-point research (n=12 stratified interviews, 4 weeks); roadmap reshuffled to surface bulk-permissions UX before account-provisioning automation; scope cut from 5 stories to 2 in the affected epic." Cites: **R10** (seniority mismatch — IC-craft-bullet variant on senior resume), **R11** (process over-narration extends to delivery-over-narration).

**Under-claim 4 — Omitting cross-functional reach.** Senior UXR resumes routinely fail to surface that the research influenced product, engineering, design, sales, support, or leadership — the bullet describes the study without naming the audience the findings reached. rubric-senior.md Criterion 3 under-claiming probe set extends to UXR's cross-org influence shape: which teams adopted the findings, which standards changed, which artifacts (research repository entries, screening templates, study playbooks) outlived the project. Fix: surface the reach explicitly — "Findings adopted by 3 product squads in subsequent quarter planning; recruitment screener template adopted by research team for similar study cohorts." Cites: **R10** (seniority mismatch — under-claim variant). rubric-senior.md Criterion 3 directly applies.

## Section 5 — Voice-mismatch examples (UXR-specific ❌/✅)

**Pair UXR-1 — Method-roster bullet (R11 territory)**

❌ *"Conducted user interviews, ran usability testing, created journey maps, synthesized affinity diagrams, facilitated workshops, built personas — to understand the user."*

✅ *"Bullet's a method inventory — six methods listed, no n=, no finding, no decision. **What changed because of you?** Apply **What/How/Why**: lead with the finding the methods produced, then compress the method list to How. Rewrite: 'Identified 3 dominant friction patterns in the v2 checkout flow via 18 moderated interviews + diary-study triangulation; team cut step 4 and reordered 2→3, post-launch abandonment dropped from 38% to 22% (Mixpanel Q4 2024).' Method names earn space when they're load-bearing — n= and the decision-impact are non-negotiable."*

*Failure mode:* method roster compounded with R01 activity-bullet failure. The fix surfaces the finding, names the methods inside the How clause, and lands the decision-impact (voice.md §11.3 *what changes because of you?*).

---

**Pair UXR-2 — Sole-influence over-claim**

❌ *"Drove the strategic decision to kill the personalization feature based on user research findings."*

✅ *"Verb's doing aspirational work — UXR rarely *drives* the decision; UXR surfaces evidence the PM or product lead weighs. *Drove* on a research bullet sets up an interview probe the candidate can't survive. Apply **Scope-Impact-Metric**, calibration not accusation: 'Surfaced 4 sessions of consistent onboarding confusion (n=12 moderated tests on the personalization prototype); PM cut the feature from Q3 scope based on findings.' Same credit, honest verb. The decision-maker named, the evidence specific, the impact real."*

*Failure mode:* R12 (claiming team win solo — UXR variant) and R10 (seniority mismatch — over-claim direction). Voice.md Pair 7 frame: positioning miss, not maturity miss.

---

**Pair UXR-3 — Hidden decision-impact (under-claim)**

❌ *"Delivered comprehensive research findings on enterprise admin user needs."*

✅ *"Bullet stops at the deliverable. **What changed because of you?** *Delivered* names the artifact, not the impact. For a senior UXR bullet, the decision is the headline. Apply **What/How/Why** with decision-impact in the Why slot: 'Reshuffled the Q3 roadmap to surface bulk-permissions UX ahead of account-provisioning automation — research surfaced bulk-perms as the load-bearing admin pain point (n=12 stratified interviews across SMB + enterprise admins, 4 weeks); 5-story epic compressed to 2.' The methods earn their place inside How; the decision earns the headline."*

*Failure mode:* R10 (senior under-claim — IC-craft-bullet variant) and R11 (delivery-over-narration). Senior-bullet diagnostic from voice.md §11.3 directly applies.

## Section 6 — ATS keyword cluster (per D-21-RR)

UXR-domain keywords surface inside bullets via honest method and study-type vocabulary, never as a separated keyword pile (voice.md §9.3 / §11.7 No-keyword-stuffing rule). The cluster below is the natural vocabulary a UXR bullet draws on; inclusion happens via bullet rewrites that name the actual methods used.

**Method names:** moderated usability testing, unmoderated usability testing, generative interviews, evaluative interviews, diary studies, longitudinal studies, contextual inquiry, ethnographic observation, card sorting, tree testing, RITE method, concept testing, A/B testing, multivariate testing, surveys, intercepts.

**Analysis approaches:** thematic analysis, affinity diagramming, journey mapping, jobs-to-be-done framing, mental-model synthesis, opportunity mapping, double-coding, inter-rater reliability, mixed-methods triangulation, quantitative-qualitative integration.

**Tools:** UserTesting, dscout, Lookback, Maze, Optimal Workshop, Dovetail, Condens, EnjoyHQ, Qualtrics, SurveyMonkey, Typeform, Otter, Notably, Aurelius. <!-- GAP: tools list reflects 2024–2025 industry stack; STREAM_MINING_RESUME.md does not anchor specific UXR tool names verbatim. v1.1+ tooling refresh cadence should track what UXR teams actually use as the market shifts. -->

**Discipline / rigor signals:** screening criteria, stratified sampling, recruitment screener, research repository, research ops, study cadence, research democratization, quant-qual integration, behavioral data, attitudinal data, leading vs. lagging indicators.

**Cross-functional / scope signals:** stakeholder mapping, research roadmap, insights synthesis, decision-impact framing, research-led prioritization, OKR shaping, hypothesis validation, hypothesis invalidation.

The cluster is content for honest bullet rewrites, not a "skills section" pile. Per **R05** (tool-list noun salad) and voice.md §11.7: trim skills to the 5–8 tools and methods used in the last two years; primary methods land inside bullets where they're doing semantic work; the rest live in role-line housekeeping ("research lead within product squad of 3 designers + 4 PMs + 12 engineers; mixed-methods toolkit including Dovetail repository ops"). **Skim-test tiers** runs first — keyword density at the top of the resume (header, summary, recent role) carries more signal than density at the bottom (skills, education).
