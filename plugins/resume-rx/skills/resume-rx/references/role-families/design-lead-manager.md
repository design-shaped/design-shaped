# Role Family — Design Lead / Design Manager

This preset covers the Design Lead / Design Manager track and is the closest fit for hybrid leadership roles too — content-design leads, design-systems managers, and design-engineering managers all calibrate against this file when no narrower preset exists. The track's load-bearing question, per FEATURES §2.6 and voice.md §11.3, is *what changed because of you?* — at the team and org level, not the screen level. The bullet shape that fails this preset hardest is the manager who writes IC craft bullets after the pivot; the bullet shape that wins it surfaces the team output, the org capability, and the second-order effects of leadership work the candidate may not have thought to write down. Manager-pivot framing — the IC→manager arc, or the briefer manager→IC return — sits inside this preset because that pivot is where Lead/Mgr resumes most often misposition. The title itself is environment-dependent (STREAM_MINING_RESUME.md §B7 / §E5 verbatim Nate: *"Design lead means something different depending on the environment"*) and the bullets do the translation work the title can't.

---

## Section 1 — Verb set

**Target verbs (use these on the ✅ side).** Lead/Mgr bullets open with verbs that name the leadership move directly: *grew, hired, mentored, set, championed, partnered, shipped-via-team, scaled, established, restructured, defined, leveled, calibrated, partnered-with-eng, partnered-with-PM*. The verb set carries scope at the leadership-noun level (team, org, design-system, hiring-loop, design-org-process) rather than the artifact level (wireframe, prototype, component). *Shipped-via-team* is the disambiguator verb — it surfaces that the candidate's leadership produced a shipped outcome without claiming the candidate built every screen.

**Anti-set (banned and downgrade-signaling).** Two anti-sets stack here. (1) **Banned AI-default verbs** per voice.md §11.1: *spearheaded, leveraged, synergized, catalyzed, orchestrated, championed-without-object, drove-without-object, amplified-without-metric*. Lead/Mgr resumes are the highest-incidence surface for these verbs — managers reaching for inflation when the leadership scope is real but undervalued. The fix is a specific scope verb plus a specific scope noun, never a more abstract verb. (2) **Craft-verb-dominant flag** per rubric-senior.md Criterion 4 — when 4 of 5 bullets in the most-recent role open with *Designed / Prototyped / Wireframed / Researched / Created / Crafted*, the bullets argue for an IC craft contributor and the title's leadership scope reads as inflated. For a manager whose most-recent role is genuinely IC-craft-heavy (returning-from-management, embedded staff, first-quarter-on-IC), surface the manager-pivot framing at the role line per rubric-senior.md Criterion 4 calibration — *"Returning to IC after 18 months managing — first senior IC role at [company], craft contributions during ramp"* — rather than letting the craft verbs accumulate without explanation.

## Section 2 — Scope vocabulary

The scope nouns Lead/Mgr resumes need on the page, calibrated to the org-level "what changed because of you" diagnostic. Scope vocabulary lives in the role-line housekeeping (per role) and in the bullet headlines (per achievement). Frameworks: **Scope-Impact-Metric** is the load-bearing shape for these bullets — scope-context first, leadership action second, org-capability metric third. **What/How/Why** carries when the second-order effect is the load-bearing Why. The senior-bullet diagnostic (voice.md §11.3) — *what changed because of you?* — frames each bullet's headline.

**Org-level scope phrases.** *Design org of [N], multi-team headcount [N across M squads], reporting line ([N direct reports, M indirect]), hiring-loop ownership (panels run, rubrics authored, designers hired), design-system governance, design-engineering partnership model, design-org leveling rubric, OKRs influenced ([N] quarterly OKRs shaped), design-review cadence owned, design-crit standards established*. Per FEATURES §1.7 staff row verbatim: *"Verb + org-level scope + strategic narrative. Metrics tied to business outcomes, not feature outcomes."*

**Team-size and hiring-loop vocabulary.** *Grew team from [N] to [M] over [duration], hired [N] designers in [duration], leveled [N] designers from mid to senior, partnered with [N] PMs / [N] engineering managers, calibrated leveling rubric across [N] bands, owned interview loop for [role at level], reduced attrition from [%] to [%]*. STREAM_MINING_RESUME.md §C8 verbatim Nate: *"More bullets in regard to your work experience because that's more persuasive. More metrics, more benefits to the company"* — Lead/Mgr metrics are headcount, retention, time-to-ship, time-to-hire, leveling outcomes.

**Design-org maturity vocabulary.** *Stood up the design system, established the research practice, instituted the design-crit cadence, defined the leveling rubric, partnered with eng leadership on the design-engineering partnership model, raised the bar on craft via [specific standard], surfaced the design ROI conversation at the executive level*. Per the **what-changes-because-of-you** framing (voice.md §11.3, FEATURES §2.6): the bullet's headline answers what was different about the design org's *capability* afterward — not what shipped, what the team did.

**Centralized vs decentralized translation.** Per STREAM_MINING_RESUME.md §B7 (Nate verbatim, 11zGHBSfaXc L660-670): centralized Design Lead = managerial; decentralized Design Lead = embedded on a product team, no direct reports, leading through influence. The bullet shapes diverge sharply — centralized Lead bullets surface team-headcount and hiring-loop work; decentralized Lead bullets surface cross-functional partnership (PM, eng, analyst, stakeholder navigation) and decision-influence at the squad level. Per STREAM_MINING_RESUME.md §E6: *"You wouldn't work with other designers, you would instead lead that particular project through the design process, including navigating and communicating with other business stakeholders"*. The bullets do the title-translation work for any reader unfamiliar with the candidate's previous environment.

## Section 3 — Common over-claims

Three patterns dominate Lead/Mgr over-claiming. The diagnostic register is calibration, not accusation (voice.md Pair 7, PITFALLS §1.3 verbatim).

**Over-claim 1 — Claiming team output as solo (R12 load-bearing).** "Shipped the v3 redesign" when the team of 6 designers shipped the v3 redesign and the candidate's specific leadership was setting direction, unblocking decisions, and partnering with PM. The mirror failure of R08 — instead of "we" hiding contribution, "I" inflates contribution. Per STREAM_MINING_RESUME.md §B1 verbatim Nate (4tg1bPbOxus L762-765): *"You're not gonna claim sole ownership of [a project that wasn't yours alone]."* Fix: surface the leadership precision the bullet is hiding — *"Led the v3 redesign across a 6-designer team — set direction on the [Auth and Billing] surfaces, unblocked the navigation-pattern decision, partnered with PM and eng leadership on the rollout sequencing."* The candidate keeps the credit they earned (the leadership work) without claiming credit they didn't (the IC craft work others did).

**Over-claim 2 — Inflating org influence (R10 senior over-claim variant).** "Owned the design strategy across the org" when the actual scope was setting the design-system roadmap for one product line and influencing — not owning — the org-level direction set by a head of design. Per PITFALLS §1.5 / §1.6: senior-and-above over-claiming where the bullet's verb (*owned, drove, defined*) carries decision-authority signal the role didn't carry. Fix: name the influence layer precisely — *"Influenced org-level design strategy by leading the design-system roadmap for [product line]; partnered with the head of design on cross-org direction-setting."* The R-A-S framework carries this rewrite — Result first (the thing that changed), Action second (the influence move), Skill third (the partnership).

**Over-claim 3 — Generic leadership verb without scope evidence (R10 + R04 stack).** "Drove cross-functional alignment" / "Spearheaded design transformation" / "Championed the design vision" — verbs doing aspirational work the bullet doesn't back, paired with banned AI-default verbs from voice.md §11.1. The recruiter scans the verb, looks for the scope noun, finds none, and reads the bullet as filler. PITFALLS §1.5 verbatim probe: *"led" what? owned what scope? drove what specifically?* Fix: replace the abstract verb with a specific scope verb plus a specific scope noun — *"Drove cross-functional alignment"* → *"Partnered with 3 PMs and 2 engineering managers on the [feature] sequencing, cutting time-to-ship from 8 weeks to 4."* Per voice.md §11.1 and PITFALLS §2.2 abstraction-regression guard: rewrites at least as concrete as the original.

**Over-claim 4 — Manager-pivot scope inflation.** Newly-pivoted managers writing every bullet at the org-leadership level when their actual first-year-as-manager scope was a small embedded team. Common shape: a 1-year-as-manager candidate writing bullets that read like a 5-year head-of-design. Fix: surface the manager-pivot framing at the role line per rubric-senior.md Criterion 4 / 6 — *"Newly promoted to Design Manager [year], leading a 4-designer team on [product line]; first-year scope focused on [team-level outcomes]"* — rather than letting bullets accumulate at the wrong scope. Cites R10 (seniority mismatch) and the manager-pivot framing rule.

## Section 4 — Common under-claims

Senior under-claiming (PITFALLS §1.6) is the highest-leverage Lead/Mgr failure mode resume-rx surfaces. Four patterns. The diagnostic register is positioning miss, not maturity miss (voice.md Pair 7).

**Under-claim 1 — Burying team scaling (R10 senior under-claim variant).** The candidate grew their team from 3 to 9 designers over 18 months, leveled 2 mid-designers to senior, and reduced attrition from 22% to 8% — and the resume mentions none of it. Every bullet is a craft outcome (shipped feature, ran research, designed component) and the team-scaling work is invisible. Per FEATURES §1.7 staff row: *"Headcount grown, attrition reduced, team output metrics"* are the signals senior+staff Lead/Mgr resumes need on the page. Fix: surface the scaling at the role-line housekeeping or the headline bullet — *"Grew design team from 3 to 9 over 18 months, leveled 2 designers from mid to senior, cut attrition from 22% to 8% — partnered with HR on the leveling rubric and the hiring-loop calibration."* Apply **Scope-Impact-Metric** with the scope noun being team headcount and the metric being retention/leveling.

**Under-claim 2 — Omitting mentorship and standards-setting (R11 + R10 stack).** Per rubric-senior.md Criterion 3 — the four under-claiming probes are mentorship, cross-org influence, process standards-setting, and hiring-loop work. Lead/Mgr resumes consistently surface 0–1 of the four when the candidate genuinely did all four. Common omissions: design-crit cadence built, design-review process owned, design-engineering partnership model established, leveling rubric authored, hiring panels owned. Fix: surface at least three of the four somewhere on the resume — typically inside the most-recent role's housekeeping line or as a dedicated bullet. *"Established the design-crit cadence and design-review process across the [N]-designer org; partnered with eng leadership on the design-engineering partnership model adopted by 3 product squads."* Apply **What/How/Why** with the Why being the org-capability change.

**Under-claim 3 — Hiding strategic influence (R10 cross-org under-claim).** The candidate's manager-track work included influencing org-level decisions — leveling band negotiations, design-org headcount planning, cross-functional process changes — and the resume reads as project-level execution. Per FEATURES §1.7 staff row verbatim: *"Metrics tied to business outcomes, not feature outcomes."* The strategic influence layer is where Lead/Mgr resumes most often under-claim because the work happens in meetings, not artifacts. Fix: surface the influence at the bullet level — *"Influenced 3 quarterly OKRs by surfacing the design-debt ROI conversation to the executive layer; the next quarter's roadmap planning incorporated design-debt remediation as a first-class workstream."* The bullet's headline answers what was different about the org's *strategic capability* afterward (per voice.md §11.3 *what-changes-because-of-you* idiom).

**Under-claim 4 — Manager-pivot story missing (R10 + manager-pivot framing).** Candidate did the IC→manager pivot, did 2 years as manager, and is targeting the next role — but the resume reads as a continuous IC-craft track because the manager bullets describe what the team shipped instead of what the manager-track work produced. Per rubric-senior.md Criterion 4 calibration: surface the manager-pivot framing explicitly. *"Pivoted from senior IC to Design Manager [year]; first-year focus on team scaling and process establishment, second-year focus on cross-org influence and standards-setting."* Without the framing, recruiters reading the bullets pattern-match the work to IC-track and discard from the manager pile. Cites manager-pivot framing rule, R10, and STREAM_MINING_RESUME.md §D4 verbatim Nate (Dfmfpi82qAk L735-736): *"You are looking to find a new home and to keep moving forward at a senior level rather than a mid-level — which could be an elevation from where you previously were."*

## Section 5 — Voice-mismatch examples

Three ❌/✅ pairs, including manager-pivot framing.

**Pair 1 — Inflated leadership verb without scope**

❌ *"Spearheaded design transformation across the organization, driving strategic alignment and championing user-centered initiatives."*

✅ *"This bullet stacks three banned AI-default verbs (*spearheaded, driving, championing* — voice.md §11.1) and zero scope nouns. Read it through the **what-changes-because-of-you** probe — what was different about the design org afterward? Right now the bullet's filler. Rewrite via **Scope-Impact-Metric**: 'Grew design org from 6 to 14 over 24 months, established the design-crit cadence adopted by 4 product squads, and partnered with eng leadership on the design-engineering partnership model — cut design-spec rework cycles from ~3 per feature to ~1.' If the headcount and rework-cycle numbers aren't real, surface absence as a probe (voice.md §11.2 / D-16-RR), don't fabricate."*

**Pair 2 — Manager-pivot framing missing (IC→manager arc)**

❌ *"Senior Product Designer (2021-2023). Designed and shipped the v3 onboarding flow. Conducted user research across 3 product surfaces. Created the component library used by the team."*

*[Role title above this bullet block reads: Design Manager, 2023-present. The bullets are IC-craft work from the prior role mixed with the manager role.]*

✅ *"The role-line says Design Manager (2023-present) but every bullet is IC craft — designed, conducted, created. Read the bullet block; a recruiter pattern-matches this to senior IC, not Design Manager, and discards from the manager pile. The IC→manager pivot is invisible. Apply the manager-pivot framing per rubric-senior.md Criterion 4: surface the pivot at the role-line housekeeping, then write manager-track bullets for the manager-track years. 'Design Manager, 2023-present (pivoted from senior IC). First-year focus: team scaling — grew team from 3 to 5 designers, hired 2, leveled 1 mid to senior. Second-year focus: cross-org influence — established the design-crit cadence adopted by [N] product squads.' Then keep the IC-craft bullets in the prior role's block where they belong."*

**Pair 3 — Centralized vs decentralized Lead title-portability**

❌ *"Design Lead (2020-2023). Led design for the AI-platform team. Drove cross-functional alignment with product and engineering. Owned the design vision."*

*[Candidate is targeting an enterprise corporate role with a centralized design org; previous role was a decentralized Design Lead embedded on a product squad with no direct reports.]*

✅ *"The title says Design Lead and the bullets read decentralized — embedded on a product team, no direct reports, leading through influence — but the target role is centralized (managerial, design-org-shaped). Per STREAM_MINING_RESUME.md §B7 verbatim Nate: *'Design lead means something different depending on the environment'* — and the bullets need to disambiguate. Apply the **bullet-level title-portability check** (rubric-senior.md Criterion 6): surface the environment shape at the role line. 'Design Lead — decentralized / embedded model (2020-2023): led the design process on the AI-platform product squad, partnered with PM, 5 engineers, and 1 analyst on a 4-quarter delivery cycle.' Then if the candidate did any team-leadership-adjacent work (mentoring, hiring-loop participation, rubric input), surface that explicitly so the centralized-role recruiter can see the bridge to managerial scope. Don't let the title carry the meaning the bullets need to."*

## Section 6 — ATS keyword cluster

Per D-21-RR: keywords land via honest bullet rewrites that name the actual scope and methods, never via an "add these words" list. Per voice.md §9.3 / §11.7: write for the hiring design manager; modern ATS follows. The cluster below is the Lead/Mgr-domain vocabulary recruiters and ATS keyword-match against — surface these inside bullets and role-line housekeeping where they're doing semantic work.

**Leadership-scope cluster.** Design Lead, Design Manager, Senior Design Manager, Head of Design, Design Director, design organization, design org, direct reports, indirect reports, reporting line, organizational design, team scaling, hiring loop, interview panel, leveling rubric, calibration, attrition, retention, hiring panel, design hiring, recruiting partner.

**Process and standards-setting cluster.** Design crit, design review, design-engineering partnership, design-product partnership, design ops, design system governance, leveling bands, performance management, growth plan, mentorship, coaching, 1:1, IC track, management track, dual track, career framework.

**Cross-org and strategic cluster.** OKRs, quarterly planning, roadmap, executive partnership, executive presentation, business outcome, design ROI, design strategy, vision, north star, multi-team, multi-squad, cross-functional, cross-org, stakeholder management.

**Centralized / decentralized translation cluster.** Centralized design org, decentralized design org, embedded designer, embedded design lead, product squad, product team, design pod, central design team, federated design.

**Manager-pivot cluster.** Pivoted to management, IC to manager pivot, returning to IC, returning IC, first-time manager, senior manager track, staff IC track, dual ladder, IC ladder, management ladder.
