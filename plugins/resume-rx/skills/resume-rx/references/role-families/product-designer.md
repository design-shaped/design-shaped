# Role family — Product Designer

Resume-rx loads this preset when the candidate's target role string contains *Product Designer*, *Senior Product Designer*, *PD*, or environment-equivalent titles where the work centers on owning a product surface end-to-end across research, interaction, visual, and shipped behavior. In v1 scope, "Product Designer" means the IC track that pairs craft fluency with feature-level decision-making — not Design Lead/Manager (covered in the lead preset), not UX Researcher (research-track preset), not Design Engineer (engineering-track preset). Hyphenated-positioning candidates ("strategic UX product designer with marketing foundation," STREAM_MINING_RESUME.md §E2) route here when the dominant register reads PD; otherwise apply role-family-alignment area-of-work label and ask the user to disambiguate.

## Section 1 — Verb set

The PD-licensed action verbs for resume bullets, sourced from FEATURES.md §2.1 (Product Designer row) and STREAM_MINING_RESUME.md §E1 verbatim Nate (*"position yourself more about the hows and the whys of the things and not just the aesthetic presentation"*):

- **Designed** — craft-rooted, junior+ at PD; pairs with output, not method roster (cf. R11)
- **Redesigned** — implies a prior-state delta; recruiter scans for what changed
- **Shipped** — the load-bearing PD verb; closes the production loop the bullet's arguing for
- **Validated** — research-anchored decision verb; bridges PD into UX-coded territory cleanly
- **Owned** — senior+ scope verb; requires honest scope claim per R12 (claiming team win solo)
- **Led** — senior+ leadership verb; pairs with team size, surface, or decision authority

Reach for **Drove** or **Partnered** at senior tier when the action is cross-functional and the scope noun carries the work. Reach for **Built** when the PD candidate also wrote code (cross-check Design Engineer preset to avoid mis-routing).

**Anti-set (banned across all bullets, not PD-specific).** Per voice.md §11.1 / D-17-RR: *spearheaded, leveraged, synergized, catalyzed, orchestrated, championed, drove (without object), amplified (without metric)*. These signal LLM polish, almost always inflate scope, and trip the abstraction-regression guard. If a PD candidate wrote one of these, flag it and rewrite to a specific verb anchored in the actual work.

## Section 2 — Scope vocabulary

Phrases that signal real PD scope. The reviewer is scanning for evidence the candidate operates at feature surface area, navigates cross-functional partners, and closes feedback loops with users — per FEATURES §2.1 PD row (Key scope signals: *Product surface, MAU, platform*) and STREAM_MINING_RESUME.md §B3 (verbatim Nate: *"This is what really separates senior designers and even product designers from UX designers and people who make things pretty. Making things pretty is only valuable when it's validated by ideas"*).

**Feature surface area.** Name the surface the candidate owned, not the company they were at. Honest scope phrases: *the checkout flow, the onboarding sequence, the activation surface, the post-purchase loop, the dashboard's settings module, the admin permissions surface, the v2 search experience.* Generic substitutes that fail the copy-paste test (R09): *the user experience, the product, the application, the platform.*

**Cross-functional partners.** PD bullets earn senior signal by naming the partners navigated. Honest phrases: *partnered with 2 PMs and 5 engineers, co-led with the staff PM on roadmap, navigated stakeholder review across legal + compliance + 3 product teams, ran weekly design crit with 4 designers.* The numbers do real work — they translate "cross-functional" from filler into evidence (cf. STREAM_MINING_RESUME.md §C4 Nate verbatim: *"Love seeing numbers in these things"*).

**User research feedback loops.** PD candidates show research as a bullet beat, not a separate section. Honest phrases: *validated through 8 moderated usability tests with n=120, instrumented adoption via Mixpanel, ran 12 stakeholder interviews to scope the v2 problem space, A/B-tested the activation copy across 4 cohorts.* When the research isn't the candidate's own (e.g. partnering with a UXR), the precision is *"co-scoped the research with the embedded UXR"* — never claim solo on shared work (R12).

**Frameworks load-bearing for PD scope claims.** Apply **Scope-Impact-Metric** (frameworks.md) when the bullet's headline signal is what the candidate owned at scope; apply **What/How/Why** (frameworks.md) when the impact is the lead and scope sits in the How clause. Both frameworks pair with the PD verb set above.

## Section 3 — Common over-claims

Patterns where PD candidates inflate scope. Each pattern cites the R-ID it most often surfaces under.

1. **"Owned the redesign of [the product]"** when the actual contribution was a sub-surface inside a larger initiative. Surfaces under **R12** (claiming team win solo) and STREAM_MINING_RESUME.md §B1 verbatim Nate (4tg1bPbOxus L762–765): *"You're not gonna claim sole ownership of [a project that wasn't yours alone]."* Fix: precision via Scope-Impact-Metric — *"Co-led the [Auth and Billing flows] redesign within the broader v3 release; 2 of 8 designers."*

2. **Round-percentage metrics on un-instrumented work.** *"Increased engagement 45%"* on a project where the candidate had no analytics access. Surfaces under **R02** (metric fabrication risk) — fabricated metrics are identified on sight by hiring managers, and they poison trust in the metrics on the page that *are* real. Fix: voice.md §9.1 escalation chain — hard metric with sourcing first, then soft metric / behavior change, then prototyped with numbers, then honest scope. Never invent.

3. **"Drove cross-functional alignment"** as a standalone phrase without naming the alignment, the team count, or what changed. Surfaces under **R02** (vague platitude variant) and **R10** (seniority mismatch — junior over-claim shape when used at junior tier; mid over-claim when used at mid without scope evidence). Fix: name what was aligned, with whom, and what shipped because of it.

4. **"We" overuse hiding solo decision authority** — the inverse failure of #1. *"We redesigned the onboarding"* when the candidate made the actual call to cut step 2. Surfaces under **R08** ("we" overuse, hiding individual contribution). Fix: STREAM_MINING_RESUME.md P-RES-03 ownership-audit probe — *"What did you specifically own here? Where in this bullet does the contribution become precisely yours, vs the team's?"*

## Section 4 — Common under-claims

Patterns where PD candidates downplay impact. Each pattern cites the R-ID it most often surfaces under.

1. **Activity-first bullets that bury the outcome.** *"Designed wireframes for the checkout flow"* when the actual outcome was a measurable drop-off improvement. Surfaces under **R01** (activity bullet, no outcome) and STREAM_MINING_RESUME.md §C5–C6 verbatim Nate. Fix: apply What/How/Why — *"Cut checkout drop-off 38%→22% by redesigning the cart-confirmation step (8 wireframe rounds, 3 usability tests)."*

2. **Method-listing replacing decision-making.** *"Conducted user interviews, ran usability tests, created journey maps, built personas, drafted wireframes."* The bullet is a method inventory, not an accomplishment. Surfaces under **R11** (design process over-narration). Fix: compress methods into the How clause — name the *single output* the methods produced.

3. **Craft-verb dominance hiding leadership scope.** Senior PD whose every bullet opens with *Designed / Prototyped / Wireframed* — the bullets argue for a strong IC, which positions the candidate at mid regardless of years. Surfaces under **R10** (seniority mismatch — senior under-claim variant) and rubric-senior.md Criterion 4. Fix: surface the leadership-scope evidence — mentorship, hiring contribution, cross-org influence, standards-setting — that the candidate did but didn't write down (rubric-senior.md Criterion 3 under-claiming probe set).

4. **Missing second-order effect on shipped work.** Bullet stops at the metric — *"Reduced drop-off 38%→22%"* — and never names what outlived the metric (the reusable pattern, the library, the team adoption). Surfaces under **R10** (seniority mismatch — second-order-effect variant) and FEATURES §2.6 senior diagnostic. Fix: extend the bullet — *"...and produced a reusable Auth-flow library adopted across 3 product surfaces."*

## Section 5 — Voice-mismatch examples

PD-domain ❌/✅ pairs illustrating where voice goes wrong on PD bullets specifically. Each pair cites the voice.md rule by name.

**Pair PD-1 — Aesthetic positioning over scope positioning**

❌ *"Designed beautiful, intuitive interfaces for the checkout flow with attention to visual hierarchy and brand consistency."*

✅ *"Cut checkout drop-off 38%→22% by redesigning the cart-confirmation step — 8 wireframe rounds, 3 moderated usability tests, validated against the team's $200M-ARR scope. Pattern adopted across 2 adjacent surfaces."*

*Voice rule cited:* voice.md §11.7 *no-keyword-stuffing rule* (write for the hiring design manager; ATS follows — aesthetic adjectives without scope evidence read as filler) and voice.md §11.1 *banned AI-default verbs* (the ❌ leans on aesthetic-coded filler that PITFALLS §2.2 calls out as the PD-track LLM giveaway). Applies STREAM_MINING_RESUME.md §E1 verbatim Nate: *"position yourself more about the hows and the whys of the things and not just the aesthetic presentation."*

**Pair PD-2 — Process narration replacing decision-making**

❌ *"Followed user-centered design process: research, ideation, wireframing, prototyping, usability testing, iteration, and final design delivery."*

✅ *"Validated the v2 onboarding hypothesis through 12 stakeholder interviews; the team killed the personalization spike based on findings, shipped a 4-step flow instead of the 9-step proposal, and cut activation drop-off in the first quarter post-launch."*

*Voice rule cited:* voice.md *frameworks-by-name protocol* (§9) — name the framework (What/How/Why) and apply it; the ❌ side describes process without ever naming what the process produced, which is voice.md §6 *anti-roast hard rule* clause 2 failure (no cost stated in concrete recruiter terms — the bullet doesn't tell the recruiter what changed).

**Pair PD-3 — Solo claim on collaborative ownership**

❌ *"Owned the end-to-end design strategy for the mobile app redesign, defining the product vision and driving cross-functional alignment."*

✅ *"Co-led the [Auth and Billing flows] redesign within the broader mobile-app v3 release — 2 of 8 designers, partnered with 2 PMs and 5 engineers. Shipped on the planned 3-quarter timeline; the Auth flow library carried into v4."*

*Voice rule cited:* voice.md §6 *anti-roast hard rule* clause 4 (kindest plausible reading — frame as calibration, not accusation) and voice.md *Pair 7 senior-bullet diagnostic* (the rewrite asks *what changed because of you?* and surfaces the honest answer). The ❌ side compounds R12 (claiming team win solo) with R08 ("we"-shaped collaborative work re-narrated as solo).

## Section 6 — ATS keyword cluster

Per voice.md §11.7 / D-21-RR: **write for the hiring design manager; modern ATS follows.** Keywords signal role alignment to a human reviewer; they are not stuffing targets. The cluster below is the PD-domain vocabulary a hiring design manager scans for. Honest bullet rewrites that name actual tools and methods used will surface these naturally — the cluster is the audit lens, not a checklist to inject.

**Tools (PD-relevant, in-bullet over in-skills-section per R05).** Figma, FigJam, Sketch (legacy signal — flag if it's the only tool listed), Maze, dscout, Mixpanel, Amplitude, Hotjar, Looker, Notion, Linear, Jira.

**Methods.** Moderated usability testing, unmoderated tests, semi-structured interviews, A/B testing, journey mapping, jobs-to-be-done, design system contribution, accessibility audits, prototyping, design crit, stakeholder workshops, roadmap planning, sprint design, ResearchOps partnership.

**Scope nouns and partners.** Product surface, MAU, DAU, ARR, activation rate, conversion rate, drop-off, retention, NPS, SUS, CSAT, time-to-first-action, task completion, engagement, adoption, cross-functional partner, embedded squad, product squad, design org, design system team, ResearchOps, growth team, platform team, design-engineering partnership.

**Seniority signals.** End-to-end ownership, multi-quarter scope, design system governance, mentorship, hiring loop, design crit cadence, RFC authorship, OKR shaping, second-order pattern adoption.

The role-family-alignment check: a PD resume that surfaces 3+ items across each cluster (tools, methods, scope nouns) inside bullet context — not in a skills-section noun salad — passes the role-family alignment read. A PD resume whose bullets surface zero scope nouns and zero methods reads as misaligned to the role family regardless of title (PITFALLS §1.7).
